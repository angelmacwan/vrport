Manufacturing

Virtual reality in manufacturing is referred to as digital design, simulation, and integration.

Virtual reality and Augmented reality are already taking over the way we see the world around us. Almost every single industry is using these technologies to enhance their productivity and get work done faster and with much more accuracy.

Augmented reality, for real

AR and VR are tools to help with each of the above elements, building even more engagement with customers. From early sales and marketing encounters with customers, through those customers’ purchase and use of the products and into the realm of customer service and support, AR and VR are becoming more and more useful.

Virtual reality, for real

VR applications, meanwhile, enable customers to immerse themselves in a digital space such as a hospital, laboratory or oil refinery illustrating how complex products and solutions work and helping them understand how they would navigate and use these new products. At Cisco Live in June 2017, Dell EMC utilized a VR application developed by my company, Kaon Interactive, for its enterprise prospects and customers.

VR and AR in industries

Companies like FORD, IKEA, FACEBOOK, BMW, etc are using VR and AR technology for purposes like manufacturing, training, visualization, and advertising. FORD is focused on manufacturing aspect while IKEA and BMW are using AR for a better user experience and advertisement.

VR and AR Designers and Engineers

Virtual reality allows designers and engineers to interact with their designs in a more meaningful way than they can with a 2D CAD or even a 3D rendering. The potential for exploring and expanding designs to better suit the operators and maintenance workers are almost endless.

In fact, Boeing is already celebrating the capabilities of virtual reality for their manufacturing process, touting its ability to improve both flight desk design and maintainability.

Enriching Phases of Industrial Design

  • The designer can easily explore and create mock-ups or prototypes to find the best ways to create a product.
  • Decision-making time is cut down as concepts and options can be reviewed, adjusted and modified quickly.
  • The result is rapid iterative design cycles with appropriate validation in the early phases of product development itself.

VR and AR are been used in large organizations for quite a few years in order to keep up with the latest trends. But for the small-scale organizations and start-ups, affording this tech would cost a fortune.

As the technology developed, it became better with a surprising reduction in the cost which makes it available to a broad range of audience including small-scale industries. Recent updates in software and hardware have resulted in more simple and cost-effective solutions for using AR and VR in industries.

The implications for small companies seeking to adopt VR for manufacturing have moved aware from the cost barrier, as the price of tools has fallen significantly in recent years.

As technology is becoming more and more affordable and practical, a wide range of industries is using VR and AR.

Some of the major problems face by small-scale industries and start-ups like “early testing, prototyping, safety, agility, and design complexities” can be overcome by the use of AR and VR technology.

Product Development

  • One of the most important points when it comes to start-ups is prototyping.
  • Concept, prototyping, and traditional design can be incredibly lengthy and resource-intensive processes. They also require several revisions and back-and-forth communications between related parties.
  • That’s before anything is even passed to production and manufacturing, which also requires further review before a final product is launched.
  • Prototyping takes up a huge amount of money and time which reduces the profit margin.
  • Using the virtual environment to simulate a lifelike a prototype can help with saving both time and money. It becomes easy to make changes and helps to visualize the final product even before starting the manufacturing process.
  • AR / VR make it easier to analyze data and visualize the outcomes in a real world. By working the data in a virtual world, analysts gain hands-on experience that provides a deeper understanding of results. It means analysts can identify solutions in minutes rather than running weeks of numbers and analytics.

Safety, a concern

When it comes to actual manufacturing, Safety becomes a concern for the company. So, proper training is a must for employees, but training them in an actual environment can be risky and hence, a virtual environment can be created to train the employees away from the actual danger and in a fully controlled environment.

In this manner, as the virtual environment can be manipulated, people can be trained under all possible conditions they may face in the real process of manufacturing.
